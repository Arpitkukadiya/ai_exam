SET-1 Questions & Answers 
1. Write any 2 Prolog Programmes
2. Find square and cube of a number
3. Find maximum number out of three numbers
4. Display addition of numbers from 1 to N

-- Answers (Prolog) --
% Square and Cube
square_cube(N, S, C) :- S is N*N, C is N*N*N.

% Max of 3 numbers
max3(A,B,C,Max) :- Max is max(A, max(B,C)).

% Sum 1 to N
sum_n(1,1).
sum_n(N,Sum) :- N > 1, N1 is N-1, sum_n(N1,Sum1), Sum is Sum1 + N.
SET-2 Questions & Answers 
1. Write any 2 Prolog Programmes
2. Area & perimeter of a circle
3. Check divisible by 4
4. Addition from N1 to N2

-- Answers (Prolog) --
% Circle area & perimeter
circle(R, Area, Perimeter) :- Area is 3.14*R*R, Perimeter is 2*3.14*R.

% Divisible by 4
divisible_by_4(N) :- 0 is N mod 4.

% Sum between N1 and N2
sum_range(N1,N1,N1).
sum_range(N1,N2,Sum) :- N1 < N2, N3 is N2-1, sum_range(N1,N3,Sum1), Sum is Sum1 + N2.
SET-3 Questions & Answers 
1. Write any 2 Prolog Programmes
2. Convert year into minutes
3. Check odd or even
4. Series from 1 to N

-- Answers (Prolog) --
% Year to Minutes
year_to_minutes(Y, M) :- M is Y * 365 * 24 * 60.

% Odd or Even
is_even(N) :- 0 is N mod 2.
is_odd(N) :- 1 is N mod 2.

% Series from 1 to N
series(N) :- series_helper(1,N).
series_helper(I,N) :- I =< N, write(I), nl, I1 is I+1, series_helper(I1,N).
series_helper(I,N) :- I > N.
SET-4 Questions & Answers 
1. Write any 2 Prolog Programmes
2. Area & perimeter of a circle
3. Pass or Fail for percentage
4. Series from N to 1

-- Answers (Prolog) --
% Circle (same as Set-2)
circle(R, Area, Perimeter) :- Area is 3.14*R*R, Perimeter is 2*3.14*R.

% Pass or Fail
result(Per,'Pass') :- Per >= 40.
result(Per,'Fail') :- Per < 40.

% Series from N to 1
series(N) :- N > 0, write(N), nl, N1 is N-1, series(N1).
series(0).

Facts & Prolog KB

Rules

father(F, C) :- male(F), parent(F, C).
mother(M, C) :- female(M), parent(M, C).
grandparent(GP, GC) :- parent(GP, P), parent(P, GC).
sibling(X, Y) :- parent(P, X), parent(P, Y), X \= Y.
uncle(U, N) :- male(U), parent(P, N), sibling(U, P).
aunt(A, N) :- female(A), parent(P, N), sibling(A, P).

Sample Answers: Parents of Bob → Pam, Tom; Bob's kids → Ann, Peter, Pat; Jim's aunt → Ann, Pat; etc.
